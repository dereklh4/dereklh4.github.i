Real-time speech-to-text transcription on the edge

As part of an edge computing laboratory class, I worked with 2 partners on a prototype app that could benefit from the edge. We decided to do real-time speech-to-text transcription. This app captures audio on devices, and uses a local edge device to downsample, align the streams, and label the current speaker. Only a small section of that audio is then sent to the cloud for transcription and then returned to the devices. We demonstrated some of the benefits of the edge and how it can improve privacy and throughput. We made use of the open-lambda and aws lambdas to show some of the benefits of serverless computing.

  • Technologies: NodeJS, AWS, Python, Linux
